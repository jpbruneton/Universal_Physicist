\begin{abstract}
We report on the current state of a proposal to unify the Wheeler--DeWitt (WdW) equation,
the Callan--Symanzik (CS) equation of holographic renormalization, and the Group Field
Theory (GFT) functional renormalization group (FRG) into a single background-independent
master equation --- the \emph{GFT--Wetterinck equation}.
The Level~I identification of radial bulk flow with boundary RG flow is a mathematical
theorem within holographic renormalization \cite{skenderis2002,witten1998,maldacena1997};
the Level~II claim that both the WdW and CS equations arise as distinct limits of the GFT
Wetterinck equation is technically grounded but unproven; the Level~III conjecture relating
a GFT condensation phase transition to the cosmological Big Bang is highly speculative and
is marked accordingly throughout.
Three concrete tensions --- the tautology objection to the WdW--CS identification,
the inapplicability of the framework to de~Sitter space, and the absence of falsifiable
predictions beyond standard AdS/CFT --- have been incorporated as structural constraints
rather than dismissed, and they define the priority computation agenda for Round~4.
This document is an honest intermediate checkpoint; no claim should be read as established
unless explicitly marked \textbf{[solid]}.
\end{abstract}

\subsection{Level I (Solid): The WdW--CS Identification in AdS/CFT}
\label{sec:levelI}

The Wheeler--DeWitt equation in the bulk,
\begin{equation}
  \hat{\mathcal{H}}\,\Psi[g_{ij},\phi] = 0,
  \label{eq:WdW}
\end{equation}
is structurally related to the Callan--Symanzik equation for the boundary partition function
$Z[\lambda^{i},\mu]$,
\begin{equation}
  \left(\mu\frac{\partial}{\partial\mu}
        + \beta^{i}\frac{\partial}{\partial\lambda^{i}}
        - \gamma_{\mathcal{O}}\right)Z = 0,
  \label{eq:CS}
\end{equation}
via the holographic dictionary $\mu = z^{-1}$,
$\lambda^{i}\leftrightarrow\phi^{(0)}_{i}$ (boundary values of bulk fields), and the
identification of the bulk Hamiltonian constraint with the dilatation Ward
identity \cite{skenderis2002,witten1998}.
This identification is a \textbf{theorem} within holographic renormalization, not a new
conjecture \cite{maldacena1997,skenderis2002}.

The analytic continuation of the RG scale into the complex plane --- across the signature
flip at a black hole horizon --- is a \emph{non-definitional} physical claim:
\begin{equation}
  \hat{\mathcal{H}}\,\Psi\big|_{r < r_{s}} = 0
  \quad\longleftrightarrow\quad
  \mu\,\frac{\partial Z}{\partial\mu}\bigg|_{\mathrm{complex}\;\mu}
  = \hat{\mathcal{D}}\,Z.
  \label{eq:analytic_continuation}
\end{equation}
This claim is falsifiable via the analytic structure of real-time CFT correlators
(Euclidean vs.\ Lorentzian, thermal singularities).

\subsection{Level II (Speculative but Technically Grounded): The GFT--Wetterinck Equation}
\label{sec:levelII}

The fundamental GFT field is \textcolor{red}{[speculative]}
\begin{equation}
  \varphi : G^{\times 4} \to \mathbb{C},
  \qquad G = \mathrm{SL}(2,\mathbb{C}),
  \label{eq:GFT_field}
\end{equation}
interpreted as a quantum tetrahedron.  The GFT action is
\begin{equation}
  S_{\mathrm{GFT}}[\varphi]
  = \frac{1}{2}\int_{G^{4}}\varphi\,\mathcal{K}\,\varphi
  + \frac{\lambda}{5!}\int_{G^{10}}\mathcal{V}\cdot\varphi^{5}
  + \mathrm{c.c.},
  \label{eq:GFT_action}
\end{equation}
whose Feynman diagrams are 2-complexes dual to triangulated 4-manifolds, providing
a background-independent sum over spacetime topologies and geometries \cite{ashtekar2004}.

Introducing a Peter--Weyl mode cutoff $k$ on $G$ --- suppressing modes with
$\mathrm{SL}(2,\mathbb{C})$ representation labels $(\rho,n)$ satisfying
$\rho^{2}+n^{2}<k^{2}$ --- and defining the GFT effective average action $\Gamma_{k}$ via
a Legendre transform of the regularised free energy, we obtain the
\textbf{GFT Wetterinck equation} \textcolor{red}{[speculative: the derivation in this
specific GFT context has not been completed in the literature]}:
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{
    k\,\frac{\partial}{\partial k}\,\Gamma_{k}[\bar{\varphi}]
    = \frac{1}{2}\,\mathrm{STr}\!\left[
      \left(\Gamma_{k}^{(2)}[\bar{\varphi}]+\mathcal{R}_{k}\right)^{-1}
      k\,\partial_{k}\mathcal{R}_{k}
    \right]
  }
  \label{eq:GFT_Wetterinck}
\end{equation}
This is an exact functional equation on the space of GFT actions with no background metric,
fixed topology, or fixed dimension assumed.

\subsection{The Condensate Reduction to Wheeler--DeWitt}
\label{sec:condensate}

In the \emph{condensate truncation} --- valid when $\langle\varphi\rangle = \sigma \neq 0$,
corresponding to a macroscopic quantum of geometry --- the Wetterinck equation
(\ref{eq:GFT_Wetterinck}) is claimed to reduce, in the continuum many-quanta limit,
to \textcolor{red}{[speculative: reduction not yet rigorously derived]}
\begin{equation}
  \left[-\frac{d^{2}}{d\nu^{2}} + U(\nu)\right]\sigma(\nu) = 0,
  \label{eq:condensate_WdW}
\end{equation}
where $\nu \propto V^{1/3}$ is the volume eigenvalue.
Equation~(\ref{eq:condensate_WdW}) has the form of the Wheeler--DeWitt equation
(\ref{eq:WdW}) in minisuperspace, with the GFT condensate wavefunction $\sigma$ playing
the role of the universe wavefunction $\Psi$ \cite{ashtekar2004,thiemann1996}.

Beyond mean field, the GFT Wetterinck equation is expected to generate $1/N$
corrections \textcolor{red}{[speculative]}:
\begin{equation}
  \left[-\frac{d^{2}}{d\nu^{2}} + U(\nu)
        + \frac{1}{N}\,\delta U_{\mathrm{GFT}}(\nu,k)\right]\sigma = 0,
  \label{eq:1overN_correction}
\end{equation}
where $\delta U_{\mathrm{GFT}}$ is determined by $\Gamma_{k}^{(2)}$ evaluated on the
condensate and is computable in the local potential approximation (LPA).

\subsection{Level III (Highly Speculative): Phase Transition as the Big Bang}
\label{sec:levelIII}

\textcolor{red}{[Highly speculative --- no concrete calculation performed]}
The conjecture that the GFT condensation phase transition at some critical scale $k_{c}$
corresponds to the cosmological Big Bang, and that GFT critical exponents near $k_{c}$
are related to near-singularity Kasner-type exponents in the bulk geometry, is a
structurally motivated conjecture only.
The Devil's Advocate correctly identified an error in the previous synthesis: the isotropic
metric $ds^{2}\approx -d\tau^{2}+\tau^{2/3}(dx_{1}^{2}+dx_{2}^{2}+dx_{3}^{2})$ does
\emph{not} satisfy the vacuum Kasner conditions $\sum p_{i}^{2}=1$.
This claim is \textbf{withdrawn} pending a correct BKL analysis.

\subsection{The Triangle of Correspondences}
\label{sec:triangle}

We conjecture \textcolor{red}{[speculative]} that the three equations
(\ref{eq:GFT_Wetterinck}), (\ref{eq:WdW}), and (\ref{eq:CS}) are projections of a single
master equation onto different approximation regimes:

\begin{equation}
\begin{array}{c}
\textbf{GFT Wetterinck (Exact, Background-Free)}\\[4pt]
k\,\partial_{k}\Gamma_{k}[\varphi]
= \tfrac{1}{2}\,\mathrm{STr}\!\left[
  (\Gamma_{k}^{(2)}+\mathcal{R}_{k})^{-1}k\,\partial_{k}\mathcal{R}_{k}
\right]\\[12pt]
\swarrow\hspace{70pt}\searrow\\[4pt]
\textbf{WdW (condensate limit)}
\hspace{50pt}
\textbf{CS equation (AdS boundary)}\\[2pt]
\hat{\mathcal{H}}\,\Psi_{k}=0
\hspace{80pt}
\mu\,\partial_{\mu}Z = \hat{\mathcal{D}}\,Z\\[8pt]
\underbrace{\hspace{70pt}\longleftrightarrow\hspace{70pt}}_{%
  \text{holographic dictionary: }r^{-1}\leftrightarrow\mu}
\end{array}
\label{eq:triangle}
\end{equation}

The key structural claims are:
\begin{description}
  \item[Claim T1] \textcolor{red}{[speculative]}
    The WdW constraint (\ref{eq:WdW}) is the fixed-point condition of the GFT RG flow:
    \begin{equation}
      k\,\partial_{k}\Gamma_{k} = 0
      \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
      \hat{\mathcal{H}}\,\Psi = 0.
      \label{eq:T1}
    \end{equation}
    A classical spacetime geometry is a fixed point of the GFT renormalization group.

  \item[Claim T2] \textcolor{red}{[speculative]}
    The CS equation~(\ref{eq:CS}) is the same fixed-point condition expressed in boundary
    variables.  The holographic dictionary identifies the condensate approximation to GFT
    with the large-$N$ limit of the boundary CFT \cite{maldacena1997,witten1998}.

  \item[Claim T3] \textcolor{red}{[speculative]}
    The GFT scale $k$ provides a pre-geometric ordering from $k\to\infty$ (single quantum
    tetrahedron) to $k\to 0$ (classical geometry condensate).  The fixed point
    $k\,\partial_{k}\Gamma_{k}=0$ is classical spacetime; near-fixed-point flow is
    semiclassical gravity.
\end{description}

\subsection{The \texorpdfstring{$T\bar{T}$}{TT-bar} Test (Priority~0)}
\label{sec:TTbar}

In the $T\bar{T}$-deformed CFT the partition function satisfies
\begin{equation}
  \frac{\partial Z}{\partial\mu_{\mathrm{def}}}
  = \int d^{2}x\;\langle T\bar{T}(x)\rangle_{\mu_{\mathrm{def}}}\cdot Z,
  \label{eq:TTbar}
\end{equation}
which in the AdS$_{3}$ holographic dual corresponds to the bulk Hamiltonian constraint at a
radial cutoff surface $r=r_{c}\propto\mu_{\mathrm{def}}^{-1/2}$ \cite{witten1998}:
\begin{equation}
  \hat{\mathcal{H}}\big|_{r=r_{c}}\,\Psi = 0.
  \label{eq:WdW_cutoff}
\end{equation}
The concrete Priority~0 calculation is to show that the WdW equation for AdS$_{3}$ gravity
in minisuperspace with a Dirichlet wall at $r=r_{c}$ reduces \emph{exactly} to
equation~(\ref{eq:TTbar}) as $r_{c}$ varies, and to identify what additional terms --- if
any --- arise from the GFT structure.  Agreement would provide the first non-trivial
consistency check; disagreement would falsify the framework.

\section{Points of Consensus}
\label{sec:consensus}

The following points are agreed upon by all team members.

\begin{enumerate}

  \item \textbf{The WdW--CS identification is a theorem.}
    Within holographic renormalization \cite{skenderis2002,witten1998,maldacena1997}, the
    identification of the bulk Hamiltonian constraint with the dilatation Ward identity of
    the boundary theory is mathematically established.  It is not a new result of this
    programme.

  \item \textbf{The identification $\mu \equiv z^{-1}$ is definitional within holographic
    renormalization.}
    The Devil's Advocate's tautology objection is conceded in its original form.  Physical
    content arises only from the claim that the GFT Wetterinck equation \emph{derives}
    both sides rather than postulating their equality.

  \item \textbf{Background independence is essential.}
    Any acceptable quantum gravity framework must not presuppose a background metric
    \cite{ashtekar2004,thiemann1996,henson2006}.
    The GFT Wetterinck equation (\ref{eq:GFT_Wetterinck}) satisfies this requirement by
    construction.

  \item \textbf{The Kasner exponent claim of Round~2 was an error.}
    The isotropic metric $ds^{2}\approx -d\tau^{2}+\tau^{2/3}\delta_{ij}dx^{i}dx^{j}$ does
    not satisfy the vacuum Kasner conditions.  Level~III has been downgraded to
    ``highly speculative conjecture'' pending a correct BKL analysis.

  \item \textbf{The framework is currently inapplicable to de~Sitter space.}
    The proposal as developed applies primarily to the AdS/CFT setting.  Our universe is
    de~Sitter ($\Lambda > 0$).  This is the most serious limitation on physical relevance
    and is not yet resolved.

  \item \textbf{The Weinberg--Witten theorem is a serious constraint.}
    The composite-graviton/emergent-Lorentz-invariance evasion strategy is physically
    motivated but has not been demonstrated by a concrete calculation of the spin-2
    graviton vertex function in GFT perturbation theory around the condensate.

  \item \textbf{New predictions beyond standard AdS/CFT are required.}
    The $1/N$ corrections to the condensate WdW equation (\ref{eq:1overN_correction}) and
    the $T\bar{T}$ consistency check (\ref{eq:TTbar})--(\ref{eq:WdW_cutoff}) are the
    primary candidate predictions.  Neither has been computed.

\end{enumerate}

\section{Open Tensions}
\label{sec:tensions}

The following tensions are explicitly unresolved and define the frontier of the proposal.

\begin{enumerate}

  \item \textbf{Tautology vs.\ derivability of the WdW--CS identification.}
    The claim that the WdW and CS equations are \emph{derived} from (\ref{eq:GFT_Wetterinck})
    rather than postulated is the core non-tautological content of the proposal.
    The derivation has not been performed.  It is entirely possible that when carried out,
    additional terms appear that spoil the identification, or that the two limits are
    incompatible.  \textcolor{red}{[No calculation exists.  The claim could be false.]}

  \item \textbf{The de~Sitter problem.}
    The Bunch-Davies vacuum as a GFT condensate fixed point has not been constructed.
    Without it, the proposal has no direct contact with observational cosmology.
    The de~Sitter Hilbert space dimension argument (finite number of GFT quanta
    $\sim e^{S_{\mathrm{dS}}}$) is suggestive but not a derivation.
    \textcolor{red}{[Speculative.]}

  \item \textbf{Operational definition of emergent time.}
    The Page--Wootters implementation in GFT --- with the factorisation
    $\mathcal{H}_{\mathrm{GFT}} \approx \mathcal{H}_{\mathrm{clock}}
    \otimes \mathcal{H}_{\mathrm{geometry}}$ --- is proposed as a non-circular definition
    of pre-geometric time ordering.
    Whether this construction reproduces macroscopic irreversible temporal flow for a
    semiclassical observer has not been demonstrated.
    \textcolor{red}{[Speculative.]}

  \item \textbf{The Weinberg--Witten constraint.}
    The standard AdS/CFT evasion (bulk graviton lives in a different dimension) is
    potentially closed if bulk time is identified with boundary RG time and the effective
    dimension is the same on both sides.  The proposed evasion via compositeness and
    emergent Lorentz invariance in the GFT condensate requires an explicit computation of
    the graviton stress-energy coupling.  Until this is done the Weinberg--Witten
    obstruction stands as an unresolved tension.
    \textcolor{red}{[Speculative evasion.]}

  \item \textbf{The target space of the RG flow.}
    The Wild Theorist raises a deeper issue: the proposal assumes the RG flow lives on a
    manifold of local quantum field theories parameterised by couplings $\{\lambda^{i}\}$.
    In the GFT context this assumption is not obviously valid --- the space of GFT
    truncations is not a conventional coupling space, and the geometric structure of that
    space (e.g., whether it admits a well-defined Zamolodchikov metric) has not been
    established.  \textcolor{red}{[Open structural question.]}

  \item \textbf{The singularity: fixed point or breakdown?}
    The revised picture holds that the black-hole / cosmological singularity corresponds
    to the \emph{breakdown} of the condensate approximation, not to an IR fixed point.
    This is physically reasonable but changes the structure of the Level~III conjecture
    substantially.  The transition from fixed-point behaviour to condensate breakdown has
    not been characterised.
    \textcolor{red}{[Speculative.]}

\end{enumerate}
