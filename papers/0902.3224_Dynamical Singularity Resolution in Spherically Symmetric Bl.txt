Title: Dynamical Singularity Resolution in Spherically Symmetric Black Hole Formation
Authors: J. Ziprick, G. Kunstatter
Published: 2009-02-18
arXiv ID: http://arxiv.org/abs/0902.3224v1
Categories: gr-qc
Abstract:
We study numerically the effects of loop quantum gravity motivated corrections on massless scalar field collapse in Painlevé-Gullstrand coordinates. Near criticality, the system exhibits Choptuik scaling with the added features of a mass gap and a new scaling relationship dependant upon the quantum length scale. The quantum corrected collapse exhibits a radiation-like phase which resolves the singularity: the black hole consists of a compact region of space-time bounded by a single, smooth trapping horizon. The "evaporation" is not complete but leaves behind an outward moving remnant.
